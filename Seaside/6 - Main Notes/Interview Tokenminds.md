## 🧭 Day-to-Day Activities of a Scrum Master

Think of the role as **delivery flow + people + system health**.

## 🌅 Morning (Start of day)

### 1️⃣ Review delivery signals (15–30 min)

You typically check:

- Sprint progress / board flow
    
- Blockers or stalled tickets
    
- Incidents or dependencies
    
- Team availability (leave, overload)
    

👉 Goal: **spot risk early**, not manage tasks.

---

### 2️⃣ Daily Scrum (15 min)

Your role:

- Ensure the meeting stays focused
    
- Encourage transparency
    
- Surface blockers
    
- Avoid turning it into a status meeting
    

You **don’t run the meeting** — you **facilitate outcomes**.

---

## 🧑‍💻 Mid-Day (Core work)

### 3️⃣ Remove impediments (ongoing)

This is the real job.

Examples:

- Unclear requirements → align PM & team
    
- Environment issues → escalate to platform / DevOps
    
- Cross-team dependency → coordinate timelines
    
- Stakeholder interruptions → shield the team
    

This work is **continuous**, not scheduled.

---

### 4️⃣ Coach individuals & teams

You might:

- Coach a PM on backlog clarity
    
- Help an engineer raise a risk
    
- Support a new team member
    
- Address conflict or misalignment
    

Often happens in **1:1s or small group chats**.

---

### 5️⃣ Align with Product & Engineering leads

Daily or frequent syncs to:

- Re-prioritize work if needed
    
- Adjust scope to reality
    
- Make trade-offs visible
    
- Prepare for upcoming risks
    

This is where your **product background helps a lot**.

---

## 📆 Sprint / Weekly activities

### 6️⃣ Backlog refinement (1–2× per week)

You help ensure:

- Stories are ready
    
- Dependencies are visible
    
- Scope matches capacity
    
- Technical debt is acknowledged
    

You don’t write stories — you **enable readiness**.

---

### 7️⃣ Sprint review

You:

- Ensure stakeholders see real outcomes
    
- Encourage feedback
    
- Protect the team from scope creep mid-sprint
    

---

### 8️⃣ Retrospective

This is **critical**.

You:

- Facilitate honest reflection
    
- Turn insights into actions
    
- Track whether changes actually stick
    

Good Scrum Masters measure **improvement over time**, not just discussion.

---

## 📊 Ongoing responsibilities

### 9️⃣ Improve delivery flow

You look at:

- Cycle time
    
- Throughput
    
- Bottlenecks
    
- WIP limits
    

Then:

- Suggest experiments
    
- Coach the team
    
- Review results
    

---

### 🔟 Stakeholder communication

You help translate:

- Delivery reality → business expectations
    
- Business pressure → team priorities
    

Without blaming either side.
