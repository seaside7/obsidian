
# 5–7 Minute Background Introduction (Clean Version)

Hi, I’m Said. My background is mainly in building and scaling product platforms across **marketplaces, fintech, and supply-chain ecosystems**. Over the last several years I’ve been leading product and technology teams, focusing on how technology can improve operational efficiency and unlock monetization in complex ecosystems.

Currently I’m the **VP of Product and Technology at Manuva**, where I lead product strategy and engineering execution. After some organizational efficiency adjustments, my team currently consists of **3 Product Managers, 5 Backend Engineers, 2 Frontend Engineers, 1 Data Analyst, and 1 UI/UX Designer**.

Manuva operates as a **B2B manufacturing and distribution platform**, where our goal is to help manufacturers increase **capacity utilization and distribution reach**. We connect manufacturers directly with retailers and B2B buyers, offering both **custom-made products and ready-made goods**. In addition to distribution, we also help manufacturers access **supply chain financing through third-party partners**, allowing them to scale production and manage working capital more effectively.

From a product perspective, one of my key focuses has been **supplier monetization and marketplace balance**. For example, we implemented supplier ranking logic where visibility was influenced by both **commission contribution and supplier performance**, similar to a _bid × quality_ model. My responsibility was ensuring that monetization growth did not compromise buyer trust or conversion. That meant constantly balancing **revenue density, supply quality, and demand experience**, which is a common challenge in marketplace platforms.

Another major area I worked on was improving **sales productivity for our distribution partners**. We implemented **RFM analysis (Recency, Frequency, Monetary)** to segment customers and identify high-value buyers such as champion or loyal customers versus dormant or churn-risk segments. This helped our distribution partners focus on the right customers and improve their sales productivity.

Operationally, we also developed several internal platforms to support the ecosystem, including systems for **manufacturers, distributors, and retailers**, as well as logistics automation tools that use product dimension data to optimize shipping and fulfillment.

Before Manuva, I spent some time at **Finantier**, which was an **open banking and open finance platform**. The idea behind open banking is enabling financial data sharing between banks and financial institutions through secure APIs. However, at that time Indonesia’s open banking infrastructure was still developing, so we had to innovate around alternative methods to accelerate financial access.

One of the solutions we built was a **transaction categorization engine** that analyzed bank transaction data to detect income patterns and financial behavior. This helped fintech lenders make faster and more accurate lending decisions. Through partnerships with fintech platforms such as **KoinWorks, Indodana, and Komunal**, we were able to help reduce loan processing time significantly and improve acceptance rates.

We also explored **innovative credit scoring**, which evaluates borrower creditworthiness using alternative data sources such as telecom usage, e-commerce activity, and other behavioral indicators. The goal was to improve financial inclusion for **unbanked SMEs and individuals**.

Prior to that, I was **Head of Product at RedDoorz**, where I led several product domains including **Payments, Partner Platforms, and Customer Experience** across multiple markets such as Indonesia, Vietnam, Philippines, and Singapore.

On the payments side, I managed integrations with payment gateways like **Midtrans and Xendit**, and connected the payment system with our internal finance infrastructure integrated with **SAP**. My responsibility was ensuring payment reliability by monitoring success and failure rates, optimizing the payment flow, and improving the refund process.

One improvement we introduced was giving users **two refund options after cancellations**: either a traditional bank refund or an instant refund in the form of a RedDoorz voucher. This significantly improved the user experience because refunds through banks could take several days, while vouchers could be issued instantly and often encouraged users to rebook.

I also led the development of **RedPartners**, which is the partner back-office platform used by property owners to manage listings, pricing, and bookings. By improving partner self-service capabilities, we were able to reduce operational workload on internal teams and scale partner operations across multiple countries.

Before RedDoorz, I was **Head of Product at Payfazz**, a fintech company focused on enabling financial services for Indonesia’s underbanked population through a network of agents.

At Payfazz, I led cross-functional teams across product, operations, and engineering to develop several platforms including **ShopFazz**, **SendFazz**, and **PosKasir**. These platforms supported small retailers by enabling them to order inventory, access logistics services, and manage their store operations digitally.

Finally, earlier in my career I co-founded a startup called **Do-Cart**, where I served as CTO. The platform was designed to help small retailers source goods directly from wholesalers, reducing supply chain inefficiencies. We built the platform using **Node.js, serverless architecture with AWS Lambda, and React Native**, focusing on fast development cycles and scalable infrastructure. Although the startup eventually stopped operations due to funding constraints, it was an extremely valuable experience in building products from zero.

Overall, my career has been centered around building **platform businesses where technology connects multiple stakeholders — whether that’s buyers and sellers, lenders and borrowers, or property owners and tenants.** What excites me most is solving complex ecosystem problems while ensuring that product strategy translates into measurable business outcomes.
