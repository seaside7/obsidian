
## 1️⃣ How do you measure marketplace health?

I look at 4 layers:

**1. Liquidity**

- Time to first booking
    
- Search → booking conversion rate
    
- Supply/Demand ratio per city
    

**2. Utilization**

- Occupancy rate
    
- Booking frequency per property
    

**3. Balance**

- % properties with 0 booking
    
- % demand not fulfilled
    

**4. Economics**

- Revenue per property
    
- Take rate stability




## 2️⃣ What do you do if supply > demand in a city?

Goal: Increase liquidity.

Steps:

1. Segment supply:
    
    - High quality vs low quality listings
        
2. Reduce oversupply:
    
    - Pause BD onboarding
        
    - Improve listing quality standards
        
3. Stimulate demand:
    
    - Targeted voucher campaigns
        
    - Performance marketing
        
4. Price optimization guidance for owners
    

Short version:

> “Either increase demand, improve conversion, or control new supply.”



## 5️⃣ How to measure voucher effectiveness?

I look at:

- Incremental booking lift (A/B test)
    
- Conversion rate improvement
    
- Cost per incremental booking
    
- Repeat booking rate
    
- Net revenue impact
    

Important:

> “Voucher is effective only if incremental revenue > discount cost.”

## 6️⃣ When should voucher be owner-funded vs platform-funded?

Owner-funded:

- When owner wants occupancy boost
    
- When listing underperforms peers
    

Platform-funded:

- New city launch
    
- Demand acquisition campaign
    
- Competitive pressure
    

Principle:

> “Subsidy should align with who benefits long-term.”

## What is Conversion Rate Improvement?

Conversion rate improvement means:

> Increasing the percentage of users who move from one stage to the next in a funnel.

In marketplace context, we define clearly **which conversion**.

## What is Pay Per Lead (PPL)?

Pay Per Lead means:

> Owner only pays when they receive a qualified inquiry (lead), not just for visibility.

Instead of:

- Paying monthly subscription
    
- Paying for ranking boost
    

They pay:

- Per chat initiated
    
- Per phone reveal
    
- Per serious inquiry
    

---

# 📊 Example

Instead of:

Mami Ads = Rp 500,000 / month (unlimited chat)

You offer:

- Rp 20,000 per lead
    

If owner gets:

- 15 leads → pays Rp 300,000
    
- 40 leads → pays Rp 800,000
    

So revenue scales with performance.


Owners think:

“I only pay if I get demand.”

It reduces psychological barrier.

Good for:

- New owners
    
- Low-demand areas
    
- Skeptical advertisers
    

---


So you must define:

What is a valid lead?

Example:

- Chat with at least 2 messages
    
- Or tenant clicks “Schedule visit”
    
- Or verified phone number

ltv = customer lifetime value, 


#


**ARPU** = Average Revenue Per Owner per month


**LTV = ARPU × Average Lifetime**

Where:

- **ARPU** = Average Revenue Per Owner per month
    
- **Lifetime** = Average months owner stays subscribed
    

---

### Example

If:

- Mami Ads = Rp 500,000/month
    
- Average retention = 8 months
    

Then:

LTV = 500,000 × 8  
= Rp 4,000,000

That’s LTV per owner.

---

# 🔹 For PPL Model

We calculate differently:

**LTV = (Average leads per month × Price per lead × Margin) × Average lifetime**

Example:

- 30 leads/month
    
- Rp 20,000 per lead
    
- Stay active 6 months
    

Monthly revenue:  
30 × 20,000 = 600,000

LTV:  
600,000 × 6 = Rp 3,600,000


 What You Should Clarify in Interview

Ask:

- What is average retention today?
    
- What is churn rate?
    
- Is revenue recurring or seasonal?
    

Because:

Lifetime = 1 / churn rate

If churn = 20% monthly  
Lifetime = 1 / 0.2 = 5 months

Very common PM formula.


Sure.

Using the quick approximation **Lifetime = 1 / churn**:

- If churn = **20%** → lifetime = **1 / 0.20 = 5 months**
    
- If churn = **15%** → lifetime = **1 / 0.15 = 6.666… ≈ 6.7 months**
    

### Impact

Lifetime increases from **5 → 6.7 months**

- That’s **+1.7 months**
    
- Or **about +33%** longer
    

### So LTV also increases ~33% (if ARPU stays same)

Example if ARPU = Rp 500,000/month:

- LTV at 20% churn = 500,000 × 5 = **Rp 2,500,000**
    
- LTV at 15% churn = 500,000 × 6.7 ≈ **Rp 3,333,000**
    

So just reducing churn **20% → 15%** can lift LTV by ~**Rp 833,000 per owner** in this example.

### 2️⃣ Estimate Lead Capacity

Example:

City traffic = 100,000 searches/month

Search → Chat conversion = 5%

Total leads = **5,000 chats**

---

### 3️⃣ Define Healthy ROI Per Prime

Example expectation:

A Prime owner should receive:

- ~50 chats/month to justify subscription
    

---

### 4️⃣ Calculate Prime Capacity

5,000 leads ÷ 50 leads per Prime listing  
= **100 Prime listings**

So city should have about **100 Prime slots**.

---

# Key Insight

Prime count must be limited so each Prime owner still sees **strong ROI**.

If you allow too many:

- Leads per Prime drop
    
- Owners churn
    
- Monetization collapses
