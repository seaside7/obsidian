---

# 1️⃣ The Core Question

When they ask:

> **“How would you measure marketing effectiveness?”**

They want to know:

**Did the marketing campaign generate profitable growth?**

So we measure **cost vs value created**.

---

# 2️⃣ Step 1 — CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)

CAC =  
Marketing Spend ÷ Number of New Customers

Example:

Marketing spend = **100M**  
New tenants acquired = **2,000**

CAC =

100M ÷ 2,000 = **50k per customer**

Meaning it costs **50k to acquire 1 tenant**.

is it increase from previous campaign, or decreasing

---

# 3️⃣ Step 2 — Conversion Rate

Marketing may bring traffic, but we need to know:

**How many convert to booking?**

Example funnel:

|Stage|Users|
|---|---|
|Ad click|10,000|
|Property page|6,000|
|Chat|1,200|
|Booking|300|

Conversion rate =

300 ÷ 10,000 = **3%**

This tells you **traffic quality**.

---

# 4️⃣ Step 3 — LTV (Lifetime Value)

LTV =  
Total profit from one customer over time.

Example:

Average booking profit = **150k**  
Customer books **3 times/year**

LTV =

150k × 3 = **450k**

---

# 5️⃣ Step 4 — LTV / CAC Ratio

This is the **most important marketing metric**.

Example:

LTV = **450k**  
CAC = **50k**

LTV/CAC =

450k ÷ 50k = **9**

Meaning:

Every **1 rupiah spent** generates **9 rupiah profit**.

Good marketplaces usually want:

**LTV/CAC ≥ 3**

---

# 6️⃣ Step 5 — Booking Conversion

For marketplaces like Mamikos, we also track:

search → property click → chat → booking

Because marketing may drive **low quality traffic**.

Example:

Traffic increases but booking stays same → bad campaign.

---

# 7️⃣ Clean Interview Answer

You can say:

> “To measure marketing effectiveness, I would track the full acquisition funnel including CAC, conversion rates from traffic to booking, and the LTV to CAC ratio to ensure marketing spend generates profitable growth.”


# Question

> **“If traffic increases but bookings don’t increase, what does that mean?”**

This is a **very common marketing diagnostic question**.

The key idea:

👉 **Traffic ≠ value**

More users visiting doesn’t mean more bookings.

---

# Step 1 — Look at the Funnel

Example funnel:

|Stage|Before|After Campaign|
|---|---|---|
|Traffic|10,000|20,000|
|Property click|6,000|7,000|
|Chat|1,200|1,100|
|Booking|300|290|

Traffic doubled but **bookings stayed flat**.

So something is wrong in the funnel.

---

# Step 2 — Possible Reasons

## 1️⃣ Low Quality Traffic

Marketing may bring users who are **not actually looking for kos**.

Example:

- wrong audience targeting
    
- wrong keywords
    
- misleading ads
    

---

## 2️⃣ Poor Landing Page Experience

Users click the ad but:

- property listings unclear
    
- price mismatch
    
- slow page load
    

Users leave quickly.

---

## 3️⃣ Supply / Inventory Problem

Users search but:

- no relevant kos available
    
- prices too high
    
- rooms already full
    

So they cannot book.

---

## 4️⃣ Pricing / Trust Issues

Users hesitate because:

- hidden fees
    
- poor reviews
    
- unclear booking process
    

---

# Step 3 — What a PM Should Do

You investigate where drop happens.

Example metrics:

- CTR
    
- bounce rate
    
- chat initiation
    
- booking conversion
    

Then fix the **biggest funnel leakage**.

---

# Strong Interview Answer

You can say:

> “If traffic increases but bookings remain flat, it likely indicates a drop in traffic quality or a conversion issue in the booking funnel. I would analyze where users drop off in the funnel to identify whether the problem is audience targeting, product experience, or supply availability.”



Short answer: **not necessarily increase LTV, but ensure LTV grows at least as fast as CAC.**

### Why

When marketing budget increases:

- **CAC usually increases** (harder customers to acquire).
    
- If **LTV stays the same**, the **LTV/CAC ratio drops** → ROI worse.
    

### Two ways to keep ROI healthy

**1️⃣ Control CAC**

- better targeting
    
- better channels
    
- improve conversion rate
    

**2️⃣ Increase LTV**

- increase repeat bookings
    
- increase booking value
    
- improve retention
    

### Example

Before scaling:

LTV = **450k**  
CAC = **50k**  
LTV/CAC = **9**

After scaling:

CAC = **75k**

To keep ratio ≈9, LTV must increase to about **675k**.

### Short interview answer

> “When marketing spend increases, CAC usually rises. To maintain healthy unit economics we either control CAC growth or increase LTV through better retention, repeat bookings, or higher transaction value.”



### Key idea

ROI from marketing depends on:

**LTV / CAC**

- **LTV** = profit from a customer over time
    
- **CAC** = cost to acquire that customer
    

---

### Example

Before scaling marketing:

LTV = **450k**  
CAC = **50k**

LTV/CAC = **9**

Meaning: every **1 rupiah spent** returns **9 rupiah value**.

---

### After doubling spend

CAC increases to **66k**.

Now:

450k ÷ 66k ≈ **6.8**

ROI **drops** because the return per marketing dollar is smaller.

---

### Why CAC usually increases when scaling marketing

Because the **best customers are captured first**.  
To acquire more users, marketing must target:

- weaker audiences
    
- more expensive channels
    
- broader targeting
    

So **cost per acquisition rises**.

---

### Short interview answer

> “If CAC increases while LTV stays constant, the LTV/CAC ratio declines, meaning each marketing dollar generates less return.”
