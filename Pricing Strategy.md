First question:

> **How would you measure incremental bookings from Gold Plus?**

---

# 1️⃣ Why This Is Hard

Because we need to know:

> Did Gold Plus _really_ create more bookings?

Maybe the property would get those bookings **even without Gold Plus**.

So we must isolate the **causal effect**.

---

# 2️⃣ Best Method — A/B Test

This is the cleanest way.

Example:

Take **similar properties**.

Group A → Gold Plus ranking  
Group B → normal ranking

After 1 month compare:

|Metric|Gold Plus|Control|
|---|---|---|
|Bookings|10|5|

Incremental bookings = **5**

So Gold Plus creates **5 extra bookings**.

---

# 3️⃣ Alternative Method — Before/After Analysis

If A/B test not possible.

Compare:

Before Gold Plus:

5 bookings/month

After Gold Plus:

9 bookings/month

Incremental bookings = **4**

But this method is weaker because traffic might change.

---

# 4️⃣ Another Useful Metric

Also monitor:

- **lead per listing**
    
- **chat per listing**
    
- **booking conversion**
    

Because the funnel is:

search → click → chat → booking

Gold Plus should improve **visibility → leads**.

---

# 5️⃣ Interview Answer

You can say:

> “The best way is to run an A/B experiment comparing similar properties with and without Gold Plus exposure, and measure the incremental difference in leads and bookings.”

That’s exactly what they want to hear.

---

# Now the Second Question

> **How would you decide the price of Gold Plus and Prime?**

This is where **CFO thinking** comes in.

Three main factors.

---

# 1️⃣ Demand Per City

Not all cities have the same demand.

Example:

Jakarta:

- 100,000 searches/month
    

Yogyakarta:

- 20,000 searches/month
    

More demand means:

- higher booking potential
    
- owners willing to pay more
    

So pricing can be **city tiered**.

Example:

|City|Gold Plus|
|---|---|
|Jakarta|120k|
|Bandung|90k|
|Smaller city|60k|

---

# 2️⃣ Property Competition

If there are many kos in one area:

Owners compete for visibility.

More competition → higher willingness to pay.

Example:

Area near university:

- 300 kos
    
- high competition
    

Ads value becomes higher.

---

# 3️⃣ Expected Lead Value

This is the **most important pricing anchor**.

Calculate:

profit per booking × incremental bookings.

Example:

Profit per booking = **200k**

Gold Plus generates:

+5 bookings

Owner extra profit:

5 × 200k = **1M**

So subscription should be **fraction of that value**.

---

# 4️⃣ Prime vs Gold Plus

Prime should deliver **significantly more exposure**.

Example:

|Tier|Expected Leads|
|---|---|
|Gold Plus|20 leads|
|Prime|40 leads|

So Prime price should reflect that.

---

# Clean Interview Answer

> “I would price Gold Plus and Prime based on the incremental value they generate for property owners. The key inputs are demand in each city, competition among properties, and the expected lead value from additional bookings.”
